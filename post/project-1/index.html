New York City's Investing on Education

April 9, 2021

The Budget

We often wonder what is going on with New York City’s Department of Education and their budget. I took a look at their data and this dataset contains information on how much money the DOE received each year since 1990. The budget for the Department of Education has been increasing throughout the years. The DOE budget recently passed over 23 billion dollars per year. All dollar amounts are in 2017 dollars.

City vs State vs Federal Government

The City, State and Federal government give money to the Department of education. The state of New York was the biggest contributor to the DOE from 1990 to 2011. From 2012 to 2017, it was the City of New York that gave the biggest contribution to the Department of Education.

Difficult Times

The Federal government doesn’t give as much money to the Department of Education as the City and the State of New York do, but after difficult times, the Federal government stepped up. We know from our past history that 2009 was a very bad year for the economy. I made a pie chart to compare the percentage of total contribution for the year of 2010 and compared it to our latest year in the dataset, the year of 2017.

The Federal government on average give 7% of the total budget committed to the New York City Department of Education. In the year of 2010 the Federal government gave 16% of the total budget committed to the New York City DOE.

How Money is Spent Per Student?

The budget for the Department of Education has been increasing, but so has the student population. This graph was made to calculate how much money was given in comparison to the student population. Now we can see that between 2009 and 2013 the amount of money given per student decreased, and has been recovering from there ever since.

Students and Pedagogic Staff Members

The dataset also provides the amount of pedagogic staff working for the DOE for every single one of those years. I made a student / staff ratio to see how many staff members the DOE had in comparison to students. If the ratio is 10, it means there are ten students per pedagogic staff member. This ratio increased after the year 2009 and had a significant decrease in 2016.

More data needs to be published by the New York City Department of Education to do further analysis by borough and by school.
